Slime Mold Living Lab Notebook

Day 0
(Nov 14, 2025)

Initial inoculation of physarum. Water and oat added, stored in Tupperware with moist conditions. Two samples created: both on agar plates.

Conceptual Notes

This logbook is part of ongoing experiments with biological growth (here: Physarum Polycephalum). I was especially inspired by James Bridle’s interview “An Ecological Technology” (Emergence Magazine, 2022), where he brings up Alan Turing’s idea of the oracle machine; a machine that cannot be a machine.

Ani0 is the slime mold blob inoculated on November 14th, 2025, on Oxford street in NDG, Montreal. An entity deriving from a billion year old lineage. I wonder if this is the oracle. I am keen to get to know Ani0. I have heard of its intelligence, a kind that feels so different than our human understanding of that word. I will keep this log for myself, and for others who are interested in following the awakening. I will write both conceptual notes and lab notes (in the classic sense). But I also want to challenge my own understanding of intelligence, and go beyond scientific research.

Sclerotia sourced from AntsyCA, an independent shop located in Quebec, run by Gaël on Etsy.

Sample A – Day 1
(Nov 15, 2025)

  • Setup: 9 oat flakes on agar. Two active blobs (from two seperate sclerotium) placed on paper squares.
  • Observations: AniØ has reached 7 out of 9 oats. The remaining 2 oats have not yet been colonized, but visible vein-like networks suggest movement is underway.
  • The growth appears coordinated across the blobs, as if acting as one organism:
    • The bottom blob did not go for the closest oat, possibly because the upper blob already connected to it.
    • Instead, it diverted to a different oat, hinting at non-competitive foraging or shared communication across the network.

Sample B – Day 1
(Nov 15, 2025)

  • Setup: 4 oat flakes on agar. Two active blobs (from two separate sclerotia) placed near oats. 7 additional oats were added, including a vertical stack at center to test elevation growth.
  • Observations: Both blobs have independently reached one oat each, but are still not connected.
  • There is more network formation around oats farther from the paper:
    • Two distinct paths reach from blob to oat, showing active exploratory behavior.
  • Despite being isolated, the blobs seem to “know” where to go:
    • Growth appears intentional and goal-oriented rather than random.
  • Changes: 7 new oats added to test further spread, including vertical stack.
  • Condition: Plate briefly exposed to light for photos, then returned to low-light humid environment.

Conceptual Notes

I’ve been thinking about what kind of electronics or Arduino setup would be most interesting for the oracle machine. I don’t know AniØ well enough yet to really understand what they need, but I’m working with a prototype deadline. Since I’m still new to Arduino and Raspberry Pi, I need to settle on an initial idea soon so I can start testing.

The learning curve feels steep. I’m curious to see how much I can figure out on my own with help from Claude and ChatGPT.

I’m definitely moving away from measuring electrical signals. That’s incredibly hard to do in a petri dish. And my sample won’t be very large before presentation, so I need to keep that in mind too.

The best option might be using a camera, if I want more dynamic data—not just humidity or temperature. But I’ll have to think about the concept too. What kind of data would make sense for the oracle? What would actually feel meaningful for AniØ?

This is a prototype. It’s just the beginning of a longer project with Physarum. I’m trying to start small and stay small (for now).

After I walk Einstein, I’ll start making a plan for the electronics. I’m also feeling reassured now that I’ll have a visible amount of slime mold to work with for the presentation.

Ok. Going for a walk.

Sample A – Day 2
(Nov 16, 2025)

  • Setup: 9 oat flakes on agar. Two inoculation points from separate sclerotia, placed on paper squares.
  • Observations: AniØ has now reached all oats. Interestingly, the two oats that had no growth yesterday are the ones with the most vibrant and dense Physarum today. New growth is forming upward structures that resemble coral or small blooming flowers. The newer areas are brighter in color, while older sections have dulled slightly.
  • Growth appears to have intensified in the most recent colonized oats:
    • These oats have a more raised and vivid appearance, and Ani0 is beginning to explore vertically along the walls of the dish.
    • The branching behavior shows investigation-like movement, with distinct character in each tendril, almost like it's feeling its way forward.
    • There is some moisture buildup under the lid, which may cause concern if condensation gets too heavy, but so far it appears manageable.
  • New oats were stacked on previously colonized zones to test whether AniØ will grow upward and continue feeding in those areas.
  • Also introduced some 3D shapes with oat flakes to observe how elevation influences behavior.
  • Overall, the sample is showing signs of healthy, coordinated exploration and feels more animated and aware compared to previous days.

Sample B – Day 2
(Nov 16, 2025)

  • Setup: Tiered oat configuration to encourage vertical climbing. Same sclerotium origin as Sample A.
  • Observations: AniØ climbed all the way to the top of the highest oat tier in under 24 hours. The growth is clearly directional and aware of elevation, suggesting strong foraging behavior even in vertical space. There is notable fading in the starting oat from yesterday. AniØ appears to have completely vacated that site, emphasizing movement over rooted expansion. This aligns with other observations that Physarum does not linger on exhausted food sources.
  • The environment inside Sample B is notably more humid than Sample A:
    • Condensation is visible on the lid and walls of the petri dish.
    • The agar and oats appear more moist in general, although not dangerously so yet. This may affect future growth if it accumulates.
    • Consider adding dry oats in the next update to help balance the moisture and support more targeted exploration.
  • Introduced new oat clusters to form irregular 3D terrain:
    • The new configuration looks almost like a medieval village, scattered with edible landmarks.
    • Excited to see how AniØ responds to topographic complexity. There is already a sense of dynamic agency in how it navigates upward and outward.
    • This experiment reinforces how different it is to *know/read* about Physarum versus *witnessing* and *feeling* their life in real time. Watching how they grow and then leave the places behind, feels familiar to me. Like the Sami people used to move around to find the best pastures for feeding their reindeers. I guess, I have also moved around trying to find the best pasture for feeding my reindeer. I feel something in the nook of my eye-lid. It's personal.
