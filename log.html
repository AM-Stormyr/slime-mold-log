Initial inoculation of physarum. Water and oat added, stored in Tupperware with moist conditions. Two locations tested: both on agar plates.

Conceptual Notes

This logbook is part of an ongoing experiment merging biological growth with speculative futures. I was especially inspired by James Bridle’s interview “An Ecological Technology” (Emergence Magazine, 2022), where he brings up Alan Turing’s idea of the oracle machine; a machine that cannot be a machine.

Ani0 is the slime mold blob inoculated on November 14th, 2025, on Oxford street in NDG, Montreal. An entity deriving from a billion year old lineage. I wonder if this is the oracle. I am keen to get to know Ani0. I have heard of its intelligence, a kind that feels so different than our human understanding of that word. I will keep this log for myself, and for others who are interested in following the awakening. I will write both conceptual notes and lab notes (in the classic sense). But I also want to challenge my own understanding of intelligence, and go beyond scientific research.

Sample A – Day 1
(Nov 15, 2025)

  • Setup: 9 oat flakes on agar. Two active blobs (from two seperate sclerotium) placed on paper squares.
  • Observations: AniØ has reached 7 out of 9 oats. The remaining 2 oats have not yet been colonized, but visible vein-like networks suggest movement is underway.
  • The growth appears coordinated across the blobs, as if acting as one organism:
    • The bottom blob did not go for the closest oat, possibly because the upper blob already connected to it.
    • Instead, it diverted to a different oat, hinting at non-competitive foraging or shared communication across the network.

Sample B – Day 1
(Nov 15, 2025)

  • Setup: 4 oat flakes on agar. Two active blobs (from two separate sclerotia) placed near oats. 7 additional oats were added, including a vertical stack at center to test elevation growth.
  • Observations: Both blobs have independently reached one oat each, but are still not connected.
  • There is more network formation around oats farther from the paper:
    • Two distinct paths reach from blob to oat, showing active exploratory behavior.
  • Despite being isolated, the blobs seem to “know” where to go:
    • Growth appears intentional and goal-oriented rather than random.
  • Changes: 7 new oats added to test further spread, including vertical stack.
  • Condition: Plate briefly exposed to light for photos, then returned to low-light humid environment.

Conceptual Notes

I’ve been thinking about what kind of electronics or Arduino setup would be most interesting for the oracle machine. I don’t know AniØ well enough yet to really understand what they need, but I’m working with a prototype deadline. Since I’m still new to Arduino and Raspberry Pi, I need to settle on an initial idea soon so I can start testing.

The learning curve feels steep. I’m curious to see how much I can figure out on my own with help from Claude and ChatGPT.

I’m definitely moving away from measuring electrical signals. That’s incredibly hard to do in a petri dish. And my sample won’t be very large before presentation, so I need to keep that in mind too.

The best option might be using a camera, if I want more dynamic data—not just humidity or temperature. But I’ll have to think about the concept too. What kind of data would make sense for the oracle? What would actually feel meaningful for AniØ?

This is a prototype. It’s just the beginning of a longer project with Physarum. I’m trying to start small and stay small (for now).

After I walk Einstein, I’ll start making a plan for the electronics. I’m also feeling reassured now that I’ll have a visible amount of slime mold to work with for the presentation.
